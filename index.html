Indian Passport: The Path to Citizenship

A baby born in the United States to Indian citizen parents is generally eligible for Indian citizenship by descent under Section 4(1) of the Indian Citizenship Act, 1955. This eligibility is contingent upon the child's birth being registered with an Indian Embassy or Consulate within one year of their birth. This birth registration is a mandatory prerequisite before an application for an Indian passport can be made for the child. If the birth is not registered within this one-year timeframe, the child's eligibility category shifts to "Citizen of India by Registration." This alternative pathway requires specific approval from the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) and can result in significantly longer and more complex processing times.

  1. Online Birth Registration with Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA):
    • Begin by visiting the official Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) website dedicated to Indian Citizenship Online: indiancitizenshiponline.nic.in.
    • On the portal, select the appropriate form for "Registration of birth of a minor child at an Indian Consulate under Section 4(1) of the Citizenship Act, 1955".
    • Carefully fill out the online application form using Block/Capital letters. It is critical to ensure that all personal details, including names, address, and date of birth, match exactly as requested. Each application form is designed for one person only.
    • Upload a recent passport-size photograph of the applicant. The photograph must be square-shaped, not less than 35x35mm, with a light-colored background, and the face should cover 80% of the photo. It should not be stapled or have any signatures.
    • After accurately entering the applicant's details, and both parents' details, click 'Save and Next'. Proceed to upload the required photo and documents.
    • Thoroughly review the generated application form in PDF format for correctness and spellings before clicking 'FINAL SUBMIT TO THE MINISTRY'. Once submitted, modifications are generally not permitted, meaning any errors discovered later would necessitate filling out an entirely new application.
    • Print the final online-submitted application form. For minors below 5 years, a left thumb impression should be placed in the designated signature box. For minors aged 5 years and above, they must sign the form themselves; parents are explicitly instructed not to sign on behalf of the child in this box. Both parents must also sign on the last page of the application form.
  2. Application for Birth Certificate from Indian Mission/Consulate:
    • Following the successful online submission on the MHA portal, the printed application copy, along with other required supporting documents, must be submitted to the relevant Indian Embassy or Consulate to receive the 'Birth Certificate'. This step is crucial as it formalizes the birth registration with the Indian authorities abroad.
    • It is worth noting that some consulates may allow in-person submission for this specific service without a prior appointment, potentially offering same-day processing. This is typically contingent on all required documents being presented in duplicate (original plus a photocopy).
  3. Online Passport Application via Passport Seva Portal:
    • Next, visit the Passport Seva website, which is operated by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India: embassy.passportindia.gov.in.
    • If this is the first time applying for passport services, parents will need to register and create an account.
    • Proceed to fill out the online application form, selecting "Fresh Passport" for a minor. Parents can choose between a "36 pages" booklet or a "60 pages" jumbo booklet, noting that additional charges apply for the latter.
    • It is imperative to accurately provide both an Indian and a USA address in the online application form.
    • After completing the online form, carefully note down the Application Reference Number (ARN) that is generated. This ARN is a critical identifier that will be required for the subsequent submission to VFS Global.
  4. VFS Global Application Submission:
    • VFS Global serves as the authorized service provider for all Indian passport applications in the USA. It is an absolute requirement to complete the VFS online registration process *before* sending any physical application documents.
    • Organize all required documents precisely according to the VFS Global checklist for "Re-issue with Passport - Minor," which is the relevant checklist for a fresh passport application following birth registration.
    • Pay the necessary fees online through the VFS Global website.
    • The physical application can typically be submitted either by postal mail or by scheduling an in-person appointment, depending on the specific VFS center's policy for the jurisdiction. For the Washington D.C. jurisdiction, submission is generally restricted to postal mail only. If opting for mail, purchasing VFS courier service is recommended for reliable tracking.
    • It is crucial to ensure that the VFS payment receipt number precisely matches the Application Reference Number (ARN) obtained from the Passport Seva portal.
    • A common procedural note is to avoid stapling any documents. All photocopies must be full-page and clearly legible to prevent processing delays.
**Important Note:** Notarization is frequently required across numerous documents. Ensure notary stamps are clear and valid.

  • Passport Application (Minor, 36 pages, 5-year validity): US$50.00 (Passport Fee) + US$15.90 (VFS Global service charge) + US$2.00 (ICWF) = US$67.90.
  • Optional Services: VFS Global offers form filling (US$19.95), passport photographs (US$7.99 for 4 photos), printing (US$1.00 per page), standard overnight courier (US$18.00). Online payments for these or main application incur an additional 3.5% convenience charge.

  • Passport Application (Complex Cases/Pending PVR): Can extend to 30 days or more (excluding VFS Global's processing time), dependent on clearance from relevant authorities in India.
  • Tatkaal (Emergency): Approximately 3-5 working days; however, this service is *not guaranteed*.

Obtaining an Indian passport implies Indian citizenship, and the child will likely be required to choose between US and Indian citizenship upon reaching adulthood (typically 18 years old).

OCI Card: Lifelong Visa-Free Travel

The Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card serves as a permanent residency status for persons of Indian origin, providing lifelong visa-free travel to India. It offers many benefits that are largely akin to those enjoyed by Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) in financial, economic, and educational sectors. However, it is crucial to understand that OCI status does not confer full Indian citizenship; OCI cardholders do not possess voting rights, cannot hold constitutional posts, and are generally restricted from acquiring agricultural land in India. A minor child whose parents are Indian citizens, or where at least one parent is an Indian citizen, or whose parents were citizens of India at the time of or after January 26, 1950, is eligible to apply for an OCI card.

  1. Check Eligibility and Prepare Prerequisites:
    • Before starting the application, ensure the minor child meets all specified eligibility criteria, particularly regarding the Indian citizenship or origin of their parents.
    • Gather all required supporting documents. These documents must be in PDF format, with a maximum file size of 1000kb. It is absolutely mandatory to upload all required documents onto the Government Portal; applications will be rejected and returned unprocessed if this step is not completed.
    • Prepare the applicant's digital image and signature image according to strict specifications:
      • Applicant Photo: Equal height and width, min 200x200 pixels, max 1500x1500 pixels. Max file size 500kb.
      • Signature Photo: Aspect ratio 1:3, min 200x67 pixels, max 1500x500 pixels. Max file size 500kb.
    • For minors below 5 years, a thumb impression is required in place of a signature. For minors aged 5 years and above, they must provide their own signature on the form. Parents are explicitly instructed *not* to sign in the child's signature box.
  2. Online OCI Registration on Government Portal:
    • Navigate to the official OCI Services website: ociservices.gov.in/welcome.
    • Complete the online application form meticulously. For minor applicants, one parent is permitted to sign the form, and it must be notarized before submission.
    • Upload the digital photo and signature that were prepared earlier, ensuring they strictly adhere to the specified dimensions and file size requirements. The physical photograph affixed to the printed application form and the digital photograph uploaded online must be identical.
    • After successful online submission, print the application form, which will include an Online Registration Number.
  3. VFS Global Application Submission:
    • It is mandatory to complete the VFS Global online registration process *before* dispatching the physical application documents.
    • Submit the complete physical application form, along with all relevant enclosures and the requisite fees, to the designated VFS India Consular Application Centre within your consular jurisdiction.
    • Applications can be sent either by postal process or by booking an appointment for in-person submission, depending on the specific VFS center's operational policies. Processed documents will only be returned via courier service, either using VFS Global's offered courier service or a self-provided pre-paid label (FedEx pre-paid labels are generally not accepted).
    • Ensure that the physical application package is sent within 30 days from the date it was initially registered on the VFS system.
    • The outer envelope of the application package should be clearly superscribed as “NEW OCI - MINOR,” and each envelope should contain only one application. As a general rule, documents should not be stapled together.
**Critical:** All required documents MUST be uploaded to the Government Portal. Failure to comply with this digital upload requirement will result in the application being rejected and returned unprocessed. A common delay is parents needing to complete their own Indian passport renunciation before applying for the child's OCI.

Fees:

  • New OCI Card: US$275.00 (OCI Fee) + US$15.90 (VFS Global service fee) + US$3.00 (Indian Community Welfare Fund) = US$293.90.
  • Online Payment Convenience Charges: An additional 3.5% will be applied to the total payment for all online transactions.
  • Payment Method: Payments must be made via Money Order or Banker's Check, payable to "VFS Services (USA) Inc." Personal checks are explicitly not accepted.

Timelines:

  • Normal Processing: Approximately 5-6 weeks from the date the application is registered at the Consulate (for new OCI based on Indian origin). For re-issuance or updation of OCI Cards, it's usually 6-7 weeks. These timelines are contingent upon receiving the necessary clearance from the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) in India.
  • Tracking: Applicants can monitor the status of their OCI application online through the Government of India's OCI Services portal: ociservices.gov.in/statusEnqury. VFS Global cannot provide additional updates until the OCI card is physically received back at their office.
OCI applications **cannot be expedited or processed on an emergency basis**. If urgent travel to India is required while an OCI application is pending, parents are strongly advised to apply for an appropriate visa (such as an e-visa) as an alternative.
  • Incomplete Applications: If an OCI application is not fully completed within 20 calendar days after its initial submission to VFS Global, it will be returned unprocessed. If additional documents are requested, they must be submitted within a strict timeframe of 7 calendar days. Failure to provide missing documents may result in the application being stopped, with no refund of fees.
  • Jurisdiction: It is crucial to select the correct Indian Mission based on the applicant's U.S. residence, as submitting to an incorrect jurisdiction will lead to significant delays or outright rejection.
  • Digital Uploads are Mandatory: A critical requirement for OCI applications is the mandatory uploading of all required supporting documents onto the Government portal. Failure to comply will result in rejection.
  • Signature Matching: To avoid processing delays or rejection, the signatures of both parents on the Government OCI application form, their passports, and the Parental Authorization Form must be identical.

E-Visa: Quick & Convenient Short-Term Travel

The Indian e-visa provides a convenient electronic travel authorization that permits US citizens, including minors, to visit India for specific, short-term purposes. Unlike traditional visas, it does not require an initial physical visa stamp in the passport. The entire application and approval process for the e-visa is conducted online, making it a suitable option for those requiring quick travel authorization.

Eligibility:

  • The child must possess a valid US passport. This passport must have a minimum validity of six months from the intended date of arrival in India and should contain at least two blank pages for immigration stamping upon arrival.
  • The child's travel to India must be for purposes explicitly allowed under the e-visa categories (tourism, sightseeing, casual visits to friends/relatives, short-term yoga, medical treatment, accompanying medical patient).
  • The child is required to have a confirmed return or onward ticket and demonstrate sufficient funds to support their stay in India.
  • The child should not have a residence or occupation established in India.

Types of E-Visas for US Citizen Minors:

  • e-Tourist Visa (eTV): For recreational purposes. Typically allows multiple entries. Validity options for 30 days, 1 year, or 5 years.
  • e-Medical Visa: For minors seeking medical treatment in India. Permits triple entries, valid for max 120 days. Requires letter from recognized Indian medical institution.
  • e-Medical Attendant Visa: For individuals accompanying patients. Also allows triple entries, valid up to 120 days. Max two medical attendants per patient.
  1. Online Application on Official Government Portal:
    • Access the official Government of India e-visa website: indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html.
    • Select the appropriate e-visa category that matches the minor's purpose of travel.
    • Fill out the online India visa application form with all the minor's details, including personal information, passport details, and travel plans. All information must be provided in English. The entire process of filling the application and making payment is estimated to take between 10 to 15 minutes.
    • The e-visa process is a "completely online application for which no facilitation is required by any intermediary / agent etc.". Parents must fill it "himself/herself" and take "responsibility for the correctness of information". This implies a higher risk of rejection due to minor errors if parents are not extremely careful.
  2. Upload Required Documents:
    • Upload a recent passport-sized photograph of the minor and a scanned copy of the minor's US passport.
    • Any additional supporting documents pertinent to the chosen visa category (e.g., a medical treatment letter for an e-Medical visa) must also be uploaded.
  3. Online Fee Payment:
    • Pay the applicable e-visa fee online using a credit card, debit card, or other accepted online payment methods.
    • The fee must be paid at least 4 days prior to the expected date of travel; otherwise, the application will not be processed.
    • It is important to note that payment status updates may take up to 2 hours due to technical or network delays. Applicants are advised to wait for this period before attempting to re-apply if the status is not immediately updated.
  4. Receive Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA):
    • Once the application is processed and approved, the e-visa, which is referred to as an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA), will be sent electronically to the registered email address.
    • Parents must print a copy of the ETA and carry it with the child during travel. It is crucial to confirm that the ETA status is shown as 'GRANTED' on the official website before commencing the journey.

Fees (for US Citizens):

  • 30-day e-Tourist Visa: US$25.00 (for applications submitted between July and March), and US$10.00 (for applications submitted between April and June).
  • One-year e-Tourist Visa: US$40.00.
  • Five-year e-Tourist Visa: US$80.00.
  • e-Medical Visa: US$80.00.
  • Bank Transaction Charges: An additional 2.5% will be charged on top of the applicable e-visa fees.

Timelines:

  • Normal Processing: Typically within 3 to 5 business days.
  • Urgent Processing: An expedited processing option may be available for those who require faster turnaround times.
  • Applications can be submitted online a minimum of 4 days in advance of the expected date of arrival. For e-Medical and e-Medical Attendant visas, the application window extends up to 120 days in advance of travel.
This is the fastest option available for travel authorization.
  • No Dual Citizenship Endorsement: E-visas are not available for individuals whose details are endorsed on a parent's or spouse's passport. Each individual, including a minor, must possess their own separate passport to apply for an e-visa.
  • Purpose of Travel: E-visas are strictly for specific purposes, such as tourism, medical treatment, business, or conference attendance. They are generally not intended for establishing residency or employment in India.
  • Non-Extendable/Non-Convertible: E-Tourist Visas are typically non-extendable and cannot be converted to other visa types once issued. This is a crucial limitation for parents considering longer stays or potential changes in their travel purpose.
  • Designated Entry Ports: Travelers holding an e-visa must arrive in India through specific designated international airports or seaports that are equipped to process e-visas.
  • Pakistani Origin: International travelers who hold a Pakistani Passport or are of Pakistani origin are not eligible for an e-visa. They must apply for a regular visa through an Indian Mission.
  • Diplomatic/Official Passports: The e-visa facility is not available for holders of Diplomatic or Official Passports, nor for those holding Laissez-passer travel documents.
  • Carry Old Passport: If the e-visa was granted on an old passport that has since been replaced, the traveler must carry both the new, valid passport and the old passport (on which the e-visa was granted) when entering India.
  • Caution Against Fraudulent Websites: Applicants are strongly advised to use only the official Government of India portal (indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html) for e-visa applications. They should exercise extreme caution regarding fraudulent websites that may appear to offer e-visa services. The Embassy or Consulate does not process e-visas and cannot assist with inquiries related to applications submitted through unofficial portals.
